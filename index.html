MonBe

"See your baby feed."

The world's first consumer-accessible, multi-sensor breast-mounted monitoring system that delivers clinical-grade feeding insights through an effortless, privacy-first platform.

$2.31B Current Market Size
$4.20B Projected by 2033
85.7% Start Breastfeeding
27.9% Continue at 6 Months

The Problem

80-90% of mothers estimate milk intake by baby behavior alone, leading to frequent misjudgment and early breastfeeding cessation.

Current solutions require weighing scales, nipple shields, or wrap-around devices that disrupt natural nursing. Only 2 companies offer genuine milk intake monitoring during nursing despite a $2.31 billion market.

Our Solution

MonBe is a passive, multi-sensor breast-mounted patch that captures comprehensive feeding data and provides AI-powered insights through smartphone integration.

The only true breast-mounted multi-sensor patch available commercially, with post-session NFC data transfer and zero manual logging required.

Technology

5-sensor fusion system:

  • Acoustic microphone (swallow detection)
  • Micro-vibration sensor (jaw motion)
  • Temperature sensor (milk letdown)
  • 4-electrode bioimpedance array
  • Pressure sensor (suction patterns)

Clinical accuracy target: ±20-25% vs reference scales

Business Model

Razor & Blade Model:

• Reusable Core: $50-100 (multi-month use)
• Daily Patches: $30-50/month (subscribe & save)
• Software: Freemium + premium analytics

Target LTV: $400-600 per customer
Gross Margin: 60-70%

Market Opportunity

Primary cessation reason: 35-44% cite "insufficient milk supply" (often incorrect perception)

Target Markets:
• US: 3.3M mothers start breastfeeding annually
• Western Europe: 80-98% initiation rates
• Total Addressable Market: $100-200M

Competitive Advantage

Key differentiators:

• Only breast-mounted multi-sensor patch
• Post-session analysis (no RF during feeding)
• Complete ecosystem (Daily/Nightie/Clinic)
• Favorable patent landscape
• Northwestern University bioimpedance validation
