The Gap: Global Standards vs Real-World Feeding

What the world is trying to achieve (WHO, UNICEF, WHA)

Health systems want more exclusive breastfeeding, earlier initiation, and longer continuation. The targets are clear - the execution is not, because feeding is not measured.

Exclusive Breastfeeding (0-6 Months)
  • 2025 target: 50%
  • 2030 target: 60% (WHA) and 70% (Global Breastfeeding Collective)
Initiation and Continuation (2030)
  • Initiation: 70% within the first hour
  • Breastfeeding at 1 year: 80%
  • Breastfeeding at 2 years: 60%

Clinical reality

Parents get told to track diapers, watch the clock, and trust instincts. Clinicians do their best - but they are missing the same thing: objective feeding data.

  1. Initiate early: within 1 hour

  3. Continue up to 2 years: with complementary food from 6 months

The "Black Box" Problem

Everyone cares about feeding, but no one measures it objectively at home. That creates anxiety, unnecessary supplementation, and missed early interventions.

The core question "Is my baby eating enough?" has no reliable answer outside a clinic. Counting minutes and diapers is not a vital sign.

What uncertainty causes:

  • Early drop-off driven by anxiety, not desire
  • Unnecessary supplementation without clear evidence of low intake
  • Delayed escalation when true low intake exists
