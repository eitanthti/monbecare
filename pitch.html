Pre-seed focus

Prove repeatable manufacturing + product-market fit on a minimal SKU, then de-risk clinical adoption via a targeted validation study and early design-partner pilots.

Why now

Consumer baby-tech has trained parents to pay for monitoring. Breastfeeding remains under-instrumented, and recent entrants validate demand for objective feeding metrics.

The problem investors care about

Breastfeeding decisions are driven by uncertainty. Parents and clinicians do not have a continuous, objective view of feeding effectiveness between weigh-ins. This drives anxiety, unnecessary supplementation, extra visits, and missed early interventions.

High-frequency pain

  • "Did my baby get enough?" is the dominant question after every feed.
  • Manual logging is subjective and noisy, and scales are inconvenient.
  • Parents change behavior quickly when supply feels uncertain.

Clinical gap

  • Clinicians lack objective home data, so they rely on snapshots.
  • Readmissions and poor weight gain are expensive and emotionally costly.
  • Hospitals want measurable, auditable feeding support.

Market readiness

  • Parents already buy premium monitoring (Nanit, Owlet, etc.).
  • Breastfeeding remains a "black box" with few scalable tools.
  • Recurring consumables fit known baby-tech purchasing behavior.

The product

MonBe is a breast-worn, modular system: a reusable core attaches magnetically to a daily disposable patch. It collects feeding signals and syncs to the phone via NFC in seconds.

What it outputs (MVP)

  • Session detection (start/end, duration, patterns)
  • Side detection and trends over time
  • Latch scoring proxy and actionable coaching moments
  • "Milk Graph" style summary for reassurance and escalation

Why the form factor wins

  • Designed for daily use: 24-hour patch wear with multiple attach/detach cycles
  • Privacy-friendly by design: NFC sync avoids continuous transmission
  • Manufacturing-ready architecture: frozen BOM and defined QC steps
  • Modular for clinical SKU expansion without redesigning the platform

Engineering readiness

  • Reusable core + patch architecture defined
  • 9-pin contact array for power and signals
  • Documented assembly procedures and packaging approach

Target performance (planned validation)

  • Volume accuracy target: ¬±20-25% day 1
  • Personalized target: ¬±10-15% week 2+
  • Session detection target: F1 ‚â• 0.9

UX principle

  • Signals must reduce anxiety, not create it
  • Outputs are confidence bands and trends, not a single brittle number
  • Escalation paths for "something looks off"
Pre-seed KPI: show reliable sessions + trends users trust, then earn the right to claim tighter clinical accuracy.

Unit economics that can scale

MonBe is built around a razor-blade model with a cost structure that supports attractive blended margins as volume grows.

COGS targets

  • Core module: ~$54.30 (100k units)
  • Daily patch: ~$2.97 (1M patches/year)
  • Starter kit COGS: ~$57.27 (core + 1 patch)

Recurring engine

  • Monthly patch COGS (30): ~$89.10
  • Target retail per patch: $5.00-6.00
  • Consumables gross margin goal: ~40-50%

Price positioning (investor logic)

  • Baby-tech analogs: $200-400 devices + subscriptions
  • MonBe can bundle: starter kit + refills + optional premium app
  • Long-term margin expansion comes from software + B2B workflows
The economics above are cost targets and model inputs intended for scale manufacturing assumptions.

Pre-seed execution plan

The goal is not to "boil the ocean". The goal is to ship a minimal SKU that parents use repeatedly, then de-risk clinical adoption with the smallest validation that creates trust.

Milestones (next)

  • BMMPP: best minimal mother pay product (consumer)
  • Pilot: 50 mothers (home environment, repeated sessions)
  • Refine: model personalization, outputs, and retention loops
  • Launch: DTC for speed and iteration

What "success" looks like

  • Repeat usage with low friction (daily patch model validated)
  • Session detection and trends are trusted
  • Clear cohort retention tied to reducing anxiety and improving routines
  • Design-partner pipeline ready for clinical SKU requirements

Phase 1: DTC (US)

  • Fast iteration, high learning rate
  • Validates pricing and consumables behavior
  • Builds dataset for personalization

Phase 2: Lactation pros

  • Professional dashboard and reporting
  • Bundled "assessment packages" and referrals
  • Trust channel for parents

Phase 3: Hospitals

  • Clinical workflow and QA requirements
  • Evidence tied to readmission risk and feeding support
  • Procurement-ready packaging, training, and monitoring

Regulatory strategy: staged and realistic

MonBe starts as a non-diagnostic consumer product focused on insights and coaching. In parallel, we build the evidence and QMS needed to pursue FDA 510(k) for clinical use cases.

Stage 1: Wellness and iteration

  • Focus on session patterns, latch indicators, and reassurance
  • Language designed to avoid diagnosis or treatment claims
  • Rapid learning cycles in home environments

Stage 2: Clinical SKU

  • 510(k) substantial equivalence pathway (planned)
  • Validation study sized to the claim and endpoint
  • Move from "insights" to clinician-grade reporting and QA
Regulatory path is presented as the plan based on internal specs and standard medtech process.
