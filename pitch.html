Pre-seed focus

Prove repeatable manufacturing + product-market fit on a minimal SKU, then de-risk clinical adoption via a targeted validation study and early design-partner pilots.

Why now

Consumer baby-tech has trained parents to pay for monitoring. Breastfeeding remains under-instrumented, and recent entrants validate demand for objective feeding metrics.

The problem investors care about

Breastfeeding decisions are driven by uncertainty. Parents and clinicians do not have a continuous, objective view of feeding effectiveness between weigh-ins. This drives anxiety, unnecessary supplementation, extra visits, and missed early interventions.

High-frequency pain

  • ‚Ä¢ "Did my baby get enough?" is the dominant question after every feed.
  • ‚Ä¢ Manual logging is subjective and noisy, and scales are inconvenient.
  • ‚Ä¢ Parents change behavior quickly when supply feels uncertain.

Clinical gap

  • ‚Ä¢ Clinicians lack objective home data, so they rely on snapshots.
  • ‚Ä¢ Readmissions and poor weight gain are expensive and emotionally costly.
  • ‚Ä¢ Hospitals want measurable, auditable feeding support.

Market readiness

  • ‚Ä¢ Parents already buy premium monitoring (Nanit, Owlet, etc.).
  • ‚Ä¢ Breastfeeding remains a "black box" with few scalable tools.
  • ‚Ä¢ Recurring consumables fit known baby-tech purchasing behavior.

The product

MonBe is a breast-worn, modular system: a reusable core attaches magnetically to a daily disposable patch. It collects feeding signals and syncs to the phone via NFC in seconds.

What it outputs (MVP)

  • ‚Ä¢ Session detection (start/end, duration, patterns)
  • ‚Ä¢ Side detection and trends over time
  • ‚Ä¢ Latch scoring proxy and actionable coaching moments
  • ‚Ä¢ "Milk Graph" style summary for reassurance and escalation

Why the form factor wins

  • ‚Ä¢ Designed for daily use: 24-hour patch wear with multiple attach/detach cycles
  • ‚Ä¢ Privacy-friendly by design: NFC sync avoids continuous transmission
  • ‚Ä¢ Manufacturing-ready architecture: frozen BOM and defined QC steps
  • ‚Ä¢ Modular for clinical SKU expansion without redesigning the platform

Engineering readiness

  • ‚Ä¢ Reusable core + patch architecture defined
  • ‚Ä¢ 9-pin contact array for power and signals
  • ‚Ä¢ Documented assembly procedures and packaging approach

Regulatory strategy: staged and realistic

MonBe starts as a non-diagnostic consumer product focused on insights and coaching. In parallel, we build the evidence and QMS needed to pursue FDA 510(k) for clinical use cases.

Stage 1: Wellness and iteration

  • ‚Ä¢ Focus on session patterns, latch indicators, and reassurance
  • ‚Ä¢ Language designed to avoid diagnosis or treatment claims
  • ‚Ä¢ Rapid learning cycles in home environments

Stage 2: Clinical SKU

  • ‚Ä¢ 510(k) substantial equivalence pathway (planned)
  • ‚Ä¢ Validation study sized to the claim and endpoint
  • ‚Ä¢ Move from "insights" to clinician-grade reporting and QA
